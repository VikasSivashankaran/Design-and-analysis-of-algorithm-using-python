Plagiarism, the act of presenting someone else's work or ideas as one's own, is a serious breach of academic and professional integrity.Plagiarism extends beyond academia and affects the creative and professional realms. It encompasses various forms, such as copying text without proper attribution, paraphrasing without giving credit, or submitting someone else's work as original. In academic settings, plagiarism is often grounds for severe consequences, including academic penalties and even expulsion. The digital age has made it easier to detect plagiarism through advanced tools, emphasizing the importance of originality and ethical conduct.Technology has played a dual role, enabling both the ease of plagiarism and the development of tools to combat it. Institutions worldwide strive to educate students and professionals about the consequences of plagiarism and promote a culture of academic honesty.Plagiarism extends beyond academia and affects the creative and professional realms. In the world of journalism, publishing, and content creation, instances of plagiarism can damage reputations and lead to legal consequences. With the vast amount of information available online, the temptation to copy and paste is significant, making it crucial for writers and creators to exercise diligence in crediting sources.
